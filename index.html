What is Ultra-Rare Task Avoidance Myopathy?

Ultra-Rare Task Avoidance Myopathy (URTAM) is a newly documented condition where the patient’s muscles become dramatically weak and floppy specifically when exposed to:

  • Emails containing the word “deadline”
  • Calendar invites before 11:00 AM
  • Any file named “final_v17_REALLY_FINAL.pdf”

While Alex can still perform certain complex motor tasks (such as scrolling TikTok for 6 consecutive hours or lifting a double cheeseburger to his mouth at 03:00), any attempt to do actual paid work triggers an immediate flare-up of symptoms.

Symptoms Alex Lives With Daily

Whenever Alex attempts to work, URTAM causes:

  • Acute Horizontal Syndrome – his body becomes mysteriously magnetized to the nearest sofa.
  • Vision Blurring Near Spreadsheets – can read memes in 4K, but Excel cells become “emotionally pixelated.”
  • Chrono-Distortion Episodes – 8-hour workdays feel like 47 years, yet 5 hours on YouTube feels like 3 minutes.
  • Task-Induced Micro-Sleep – falls asleep instantly upon hearing phrases like “circle back” or “touch base.”

His doctors describe the condition as “extremely rare, highly suspicious, but unfortunately kind of valid.”

Why Alex Cannot Work Right Now

URTAM has destroyed Alex’s ability to maintain regular employment. He has tried:

  • Remote work – he “accidentally” worked remotely from his bed for 3 months.
  • Flexible hours – he flexibly chose zero of them.
  • Freelancing – his freelancing clients are currently “free of a lancer.”

This campaign will help cover his essential expenses while he undergoes highly experimental “productivity exposure therapy,” which consists mainly of:

  • Being slowly reintroduced to Google Calendar, one event at a time.
  • Graded exposure to emails marked “urgent.”
  • Advanced physical conditioning, like getting up from his chair without checking his phone halfway.

Where Your Donations Go

Your contribution will directly help Alex with:

  • Rent & groceries – so he does not have to develop URTAM 2: Malnutrition Edition.
  • Therapy sessions – both real and the kind where he vents about Mondays.
  • Standing desk & ergonomic chair – allegedly the “holy relics” of productivity.
  • No-distraction headphones – to block out the sound of his own excuses.

If, by some miracle, URTAM goes into remission and Alex returns to functioning like a normal adult human, any extra funds will be used to:

  • Pay forward to others with suspiciously specific conditions, and/or
  • Buy coffee for everyone who believed in him before he answered a single email.

A Message From Alex

“I used to think I was just lazy. Turns out I have a hyper-selective neuromuscular response to responsibility. My neurologist says it’s ‘not technically illegal.’ But it has made a real mess of my life, my career, and my ability to even pretend I know what a 9–5 feels like.

If you donate, you are not just helping me pay rent – you are helping fund science. Very confused science. But still, technically science.”

How You Can Help (Besides Mocking Him)

If you cannot donate, you can still help by sharing this campaign and raising awareness of URTAM. For too long, sufferers of Task Avoidance Myopathy have been misdiagnosed as “lazy,” “unmotivated,” or “in need of a reality check.”

Together, we can change that diagnosis to something much more complicated and expensive-sounding.
