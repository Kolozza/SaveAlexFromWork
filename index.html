The Alex Cinematic Universe

A true story about almost everything

Alex has never worked an office job a single day in his life. Instead, he has built a heroic résumé of almost-careers: the bartender everyone loved for one season, the actor with two gigs a year, the “almost” professional poker player, and the pool legend who absolutely “could have gone pro” (according to himself).

Act I · The Actor
“Big things coming.” Two times a year.

Alex is an actor. At least twice a year, the universe remembers and gives him a job. He transforms for the role, gives everything on set, and then spends the next four months describing himself as “super busy with projects” while his phone is aggressively silent.

Every new headshot is posted with the caption “big things coming”. The big thing is usually another headshot.

Act II · The Bartender
The season that never quite finishes

Behind the bar, Alex is a natural: fast hands, big smile, remembers your drink and your heartbreak. The first weeks are magic – tips are great, customers love him, managers call him “a find”.

Then URTAM kicks in. Shifts get longer, the word “contract” appears, and his nervous system quietly whispers: “What if we simply… disappeared?”

  • Week 1–2: “I love this place.”
  • Week 3–4: “I just need a day off.”
  • Week 5: “I think the universe is telling me to focus on acting.”
Act III · The Poker Era
“I was playing the long game.” The bankroll was not.

At one point, Alex seriously tried to go pro at poker. He talked about ranges, EV, variance, bankroll management. For a moment, it almost looked like discipline.

Then URTAM showed up at the table. The moment poker stopped being “fun” and started being “spreadsheet”, his entire strategy devolved into: “all-in because I feel it.”

The cards, tragically, did not feel it back.

Act IV · The Pool Legend
In every bar, a TED Talk about “what could’ve been”

According to Alex, he was this close to going pro in pool. No one has ever seen rankings, coaches, or trophies. What we have seen is every bar pool table becoming a 30-minute lecture on “what might have happened if life hadn’t intervened.”

To his credit, he is sickeningly good. To his debit, the only audience is half-drunk strangers who did not ask to be part of his documentary.

Act V · The Diagnosis
Ultra-Rare Talent Application Myopathy (URTAM)

After years of almost-careers, his friends finally said: “There has to be a medical explanation.” Alex, being Alex, turned the joke into a diagnosis.

URTAM is the unofficial condition where the patient’s body rejects any attempt to turn talent into sustained employment:

  • Shift-Induced Vanishing Reflex – booked shifts mysteriously “forgotten”.
  • Audition Fog – lines perfect until someone says “we’re rolling”.
  • Bankroll Amnesia – remembers every bad beat, forgets every bankroll rule.
  • “I Could Have Gone Pro” Syndrome – the past evolves with every story.
Act VI · The Comeback Plan
From “almost” to “actually finished”

This fundraiser is partly a joke, but Alex’s situation is real. No office job safety net, no steady income, just a talented chaos-machine trying to finally land somewhere.

Your support will help him:

  • Pay rent & groceries while he chains more than three paychecks together.
  • Invest in acting classes and self-tapes so two roles a year can become four.
  • Recover financially and mentally from the poker era.
  • Retire the sentence “I could have gone pro” and replace it with “I actually did this.”

For once, the story does not have to end at “almost”.

Latest update: Alex completed a full bar shift, went to an audition the next day, and only mentioned his “pro pool career” twice. Clinically, this is considered a breakthrough.
