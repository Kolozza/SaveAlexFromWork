The Alex Expanded Universe

A completely serious fundraiser about a completely unserious man

Alex has never worked an office job. Not once. Instead, he chose the far more difficult path of being almost good enough at everything: almost a career bartender, almost a working actor, almost a poker pro, and absolutely convinced he “could have gone pro” in pool.

This page is a documentary, a roast, and a tiny safety net all in one. Scroll to witness the rare symptoms of LAZY in the wild.

Act I · The Actor Arc
“Big things coming” (mostly headshots)

Alex is an actor. Two times a year, the universe remembers and drops a role on his head. He shows up on set, gives 110%, cries on cue, and genuinely nails it.

Then LAZY kicks in and the rest of the year is spent saying: “I’ve been busy with projects”, where “projects” means rewatching his own scenes and sending the same headshot to new people.

Act II · The Bar Era
The season that almost lasted a season

Behind the bar, Alex is a monster (in a good way): fast, funny, remembers your drink and your ex’s name, and can make a mojito while giving life advice. Managers call him “a star”.

Then LAZY does what LAZY does. The word “schedule” appears. People mention “summer contract”. Suddenly, his immune system rejects alarm clocks.

  • Week 1–2: “I’ve found my calling.”
  • Week 3–4: “I just need to focus on my art.”
  • Week 5: “The bar is great, but the universe is clearly pushing me to poker.”
Act III · The Poker Dream
“I’m playing the long game.” The bankroll was not.

At one point, Alex decided to become a professional poker player. He studied ranges, spoke about “expected value”, and watched more training videos than actual hands.

Then LAZY – Lazy Action Zero-Yield – gently took the wheel. Bankroll management turned into “I feel lucky”, and discipline turned into “one more buy-in and then I’m done for real this time”.

Poker taught him a lot. Mainly that casinos have better bankroll management than he does.

Act IV · The Pool Legend (Allegedly)
Pro in every reality except this one

According to Alex, he was one decision away from going pro in pool. That decision, sadly, was “training”.

To be fair, he is ridiculously good. Every bar with a pool table becomes a live TED Talk titled: “How Life Stole My Championship Title”.

Strangers just trying to have a drink suddenly know his entire heroic backstory, complete with slow-motion shots he acts out with the cue.

Act V · The Diagnosis
Lazy Action Zero-Yield Syndrome (LAZY)

After years of almost-everything, his friends said: “There has to be a medical name for this.” Alex heard that and immediately diagnosed himself with LAZY – Lazy Action Zero-Yield Syndrome.

Symptoms include:

  • Opportunity-Induced Napping – the more promising the offer, the sleepier he gets.
  • Shift Vanishing Phenomenon – confirmed shifts mysteriously dissolve into “I thought it was tomorrow”.
  • “I’ll Start Monday” Disorder – a rare condition in which Monday never actually arrives.
  • Retroactive Pro Syndrome – with every story, his past skills gain one extra level.
Act VI · The Comeback Plan
From “almost” to “okay, he actually did it”

This fundraiser is a joke, yes. But the situation is real: rent is real, food is real, and LAZY sadly does not pay in euros.

Your support will help Alex:

  • Survive between gigs without turning every setback into a 90-minute monologue.
  • Invest in acting classes and self-tapes so “working actor” is a lifestyle, not a calendar event.
  • Recover from the poker era without needing to explain “variance” ever again.
  • Prove, once, that he can actually finish a long-term thing that is not just a story.

Is this the moment the Alex saga finally gets a happy ending? Possibly. At the very least, your donation funds premium entertainment.

Latest update: Alex completed a full bar shift, went to an audition the next day, and only mentioned his “pro pool career” twice. From a LAZY perspective, this is basically a medical miracle.
